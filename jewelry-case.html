About: Using aluminum, acrylic, and wood, I designed and manufactured my own jewelry stand, an homage to my heritage and family name. View my final project and process photos below:

Process:

Using Adobe Illustrator, I cut out rastered my design on wood using lasercutter and adhered it to a 3D printed case. I carved the gate from scrap wood and attached it to the side of the pattern for the molten aluminum to pour through.
Here I am "ramming up," meaning I am packing sand around my pattern.
After ramming up, this is one side of my pattern. Metal will be poured in through the gate and fill the cavity, taking on the shape of my design!
A failed pour :(
After I was satisfied with my cast, I cut off the gate with a bandsaw and sanded and polished my piece.
Here I am using the mill to drill and tap screw holes to attach the hinges for my jewelry stand to fold.
